Why Project Managers Feel Burnt Out

Project management is often described as a structured, well-defined role. But many project managers experience constant exhaustion that goes far beyond timelines and tasks.

The Invisible Pressure

PMs manage delivery, expectations, risks, and emotions — often without formal authority.

Why Burnout Is Increasing

Faster delivery cycles, constant meetings, and growing stakeholder pressure have made burnout more common than ever.

What Needs to Change

Burnout is not a personal failure. It is a system problem that requires better tools, clearer roles, and healthier delivery cultures.
